PROJECT

AGRO RECOMMENDATION SYSTEM

ABSTRACT

Deep CNN models are crucial for early crop disease detection, leveraging their success in machine vision. To optimize efficiency, we substituted standard convolutions with depth separable convolutions, cutting down on parameters and computational demands. These models were trained on a diverse dataset featuring 14 plant species and 38 disease categories alongside healthy leaves. This approach enhances the timely identification and prevention of crop diseases, ultimately boosting agricultural productivity.

MAIN OBJECTIVES

This project aims to use CNN and K-Means clustering to identify and diagnose plant diseases using trained and test datasets. It focuses on early disease detection and proposes remedial measures for affected crops, thereby improving agricultural outcomes. The goal is to enhance plant health management through advanced computational techniques, ensuring timely intervention and effective disease control strategies.

EXISTING SYSTEM

Traditional logical reasoning for pest diagnosis and control has limitations in learning ability, data utilization, and accuracy, hampering practical applicability. Fine-grained tasks involve objects with subtle inter-class and significant intra-class variations, posing complex challenges. Increasing data on diseases and pests leads to issues like missing information and extended processing times. A method requiring minimal memory can achieve real-time identification of plant leaf diseases under low-performance conditions.

PROPOSED SYSTEM

Plants face diverse disease disorders influenced by environmental factors like temperature, humidity, and nutrient levels, alongside bacterial, viral, and fungal infections. Our system employs the depth separable convolution algorithm for accurate plant disease detection using CNNs, ensuring optimal performance with quality data. Dataset partitioning into phased training and testing sets mitigates overfitting, enhancing model reliability and effectiveness in diagnosing plant health issues..
